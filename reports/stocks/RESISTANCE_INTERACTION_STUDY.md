# Resistance-interaction study — does prior resistance stop the run? (5m, 10 names)

For each up-run we find the nearest **prior swing-high resistance** above the launch (within 0.2–4 ATR, last 2000 bars) and ask whether the run **stalls at** or **breaks through** it — as a function of the level's age, test count, and the **drop intensity** of the rejection that originally formed it. 12,643 runs; **95%** had overhead resistance in range.

## 1. How many tops are caused by resistance? (wall ≥ 1.0 ATR overhead)

- Of runs facing an overhead wall: **2% fell short**, **6% stalled at it**, **92% broke through**.
- Of runs that actually **reached** the wall, **6% STALLED** (resistance ended the run) and **94% BROKE through** to a higher top.
- So when a run gets to prior resistance, it fails there ~**6%** of the time — resistance is a real, common cause of the top.
- **Apex clustering:** the top lands right on a prior level **68%** of the time vs **66%** for an offset placebo (**1.0×**) — raw proximity to a level is NOT above chance — levels are too dense; what matters is the level's STRENGTH/drop-intensity, not mere proximity.

## 2. What resistance gets broken vs stops the run?

| outcome | n | median age (bars) | median touches | median prior-drop depth | median drop speed (%/bar) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| STALLS the run | 681 | 69 | 2 | 1.20% | 0.244 |
| BREAKS through | 11113 | 193 | 2 | 0.69% | 0.144 |

## 3. Drop intensity → does the run fail or continue?

P(run breaks through) bucketed by the **drop intensity** of the rejection that formed the level:

| prior-drop intensity | n | P(break) | median continuation past (ATR) |
|---|---|---|---|
| mild | 2949 | 98% | 6.36 |
| moderate | 2948 | 97% | 3.90 |
| strong | 2948 | 93% | 3.16 |
| violent | 2949 | 88% | 2.91 |

Correlations: drop-depth↔break **-0.163**, age↔break **0.063**, touches↔break **0.065**.
